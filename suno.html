What is Suno?

Suno is an AI music and song generator. It allows you to create original, high-quality songs—often including vocals, lyrics, and instrumentation—from simple text prompts. You can describe a mood, a genre, a topic, or even provide your own lyrics and have the AI turn it into a complete song.

Common Use Cases

  • Songwriting & Prototyping: Quickly turn a lyrical idea into a fully produced song to see how it sounds.
  • Custom Music: Create royalty-free background music for videos, podcasts, or presentations.
  • Creative Fun: Generate humorous songs about any topic, create personalized birthday songs, or simply experiment with music creation.
  • Inspiration: Generate musical ideas and motifs to overcome creative blocks.

Getting Started: A Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Go to the Website: Navigate to the Suno website and sign in.
  2. Describe Your Song: In the main creation box, type a description of the song you want to make. Be descriptive! For example: "A soulful acoustic pop song about a rainy day in the city."
  3. (Optional) Use Custom Mode: Toggle to "Custom Mode" to provide your own lyrics and have more control over the song's style and title.
  4. Generate: Click the "Create" button. Suno will generate two different versions of your song for you to listen to.
  5. Listen and Extend: Listen to the clips. If you like one, you can click "Extend" to continue the song, building it out verse by verse. You can also remix, download, or share your creations.

Tips for Effective Use

  • Use Genre Tags: Be specific about the musical genre. Use tags like [acoustic pop], [90s hip-hop], [cinematic orchestral] in your prompts to guide the AI.
  • Describe the Vibe: Use emotional and descriptive words for the mood, such as "somber," "energetic," "epic," or "lo-fi chill."
  • Iterate on Lyrics: In Custom Mode, if the AI doesn't sing the lyrics quite right, try rephrasing them or adding annotations like `[verse]` or `[chorus]` to guide the structure.
  • Experiment with Random Styles: If you're feeling stuck, just put in some lyrics and let Suno choose a random style. The results can be surprisingly good and lead to new ideas.
