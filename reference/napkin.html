What is Napkin?

Napkin is a unique tool for thought that helps you collect ideas and, more importantly, discover the connections between them. Instead of organizing notes in folders, Napkin uses AI to create a dynamic, networked view of your thoughts, surfacing related ideas automatically. It's designed for inspiration and "serendipitous discovery."

Common Use Cases

  • Creative Brainstorming: A place to dump all your shower thoughts and let the AI help you see the patterns.
  • Research and Learning: Collect notes from articles, books, and lectures, and let Napkin show you how different concepts are related.
  • Writing: Use it as an "idea farm." When you're ready to write, you can explore the network of thoughts you've built on a topic.
  • Personal Development: Track personal insights, quotes, and reflections, and see how your thinking evolves over time.

"Your ideas in conversation with each other."

Getting Started: A Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Capture an Idea: The core action in Napkin is adding a thought. Keep it brief and focused on a single concept. You can type it directly into the app or use integrations to send notes from other apps.
  2. Let the AI Work: As soon as you enter a note, Napkin's AI analyzes it. It doesn't just store it; it immediately looks for connections to your existing notes based on semantic meaning, not just keywords.
  3. Explore Connections: When you look at a note, Napkin will show you a "stack" of related notes underneath it. This is the magic of the app. You can browse these connections to see how your ideas relate in surprising ways.
  4. Look for "Sparks of Insight": The app will sometimes proactively show you connections you might have missed, helping you form new, more complex ideas from your simpler building blocks.
  5. Don't Organize: Resist the urge to create folders or complex tag systems. The entire philosophy of Napkin is to let the structure emerge naturally through the AI-driven connections. Just focus on collecting your thoughts.

Tips for Effective Use

  • Keep Notes Atomic: Each note should be about one thing. This makes it easier for the AI to find precise connections. Instead of a long note about a book, create several small notes about the individual ideas within it.
  • Use Your Own Words: Write thoughts in the way you naturally think them. The semantic AI is good at understanding meaning, so you don't need to use formal or keyword-heavy language.
  • Review Daily: Spend a few minutes each day looking at the connections Napkin has surfaced. This is a great way to spark new ideas or see your work from a different perspective.
  • Trust the Process: It can feel strange not having a folder structure. Trust that by focusing on collection, the important ideas and connections will surface when you need them.
