What is Perplexity AI?

Perplexity AI is an AI-powered "answer engine" that combines a large language model with a search engine. Instead of just giving you a list of links, it directly answers your questions and provides a list of sources and citations for its claims, making it a powerful tool for research.

Common Use Cases

  • Academic Research: Quickly find papers, get summaries of complex topics, and gather citations for a bibliography.
  • Fact-Checking: Verify claims you see online by asking Perplexity for a sourced answer.
  • Learning Something New: Get a quick, reliable overview of any topic, from historical events to scientific theories.
  • Professional Research: Summarize market trends, analyze competitor reports, or understand new regulations, all with cited sources.

Getting Started: A Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Go to the Website: Navigate to the Perplexity AI website. The interface is clean and search-focused.
  2. Ask a Question: Type a question into the search bar just as you would on Google, but you can be more conversational. For example: "What are the pros and cons of nuclear energy compared to solar power?"
  3. Review the Answer and Sources: Perplexity will write a comprehensive answer, often with headings and bullet points. Crucially, you will see numbered citations next to the claims. Below the answer, you'll find a list of the sources it used.
  4. Ask Follow-up Questions: The initial answer is just the start. You can ask clarifying questions in the same thread, and Perplexity will maintain the context of the conversation.
  5. Use the "Focus" Feature: For more targeted searches, you can use the "Focus" setting to limit the search to specific domains like Academic papers, YouTube, Reddit, or WolframAlpha for computational knowledge.

Tips for Effective Use

  • Always Check the Sources: The biggest advantage of Perplexity is its transparency. Click on the source links to verify the information and get more context.
  • Use "Copilot" for Deeper Research: The pro version includes a feature called Copilot, which asks you clarifying questions to better understand your intent and conduct a more thorough search.
  • Attach Files: You can upload documents (like PDFs or text files) and ask questions about them, turning Perplexity into a research assistant for your own material.
  • Think of it as a Research Partner: Use it to get a foundational understanding of a topic, complete with sources, before diving deeper into the primary materials.
