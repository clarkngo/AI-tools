What is Consensus?

Consensus is an AI-powered search engine designed specifically for scientific research. Instead of searching through blogs and news articles, it searches a database of over 200 million peer-reviewed papers. It uses AI to extract the key findings related to your question and presents them in a clear, digestible format, making it easy to find evidence-based answers.

Common Use Cases

  • Academic Research: Quickly find out what the scientific literature says about a specific topic, hypothesis, or relationship between variables.
  • Fact-Checking: Verify health, science, or social science claims by going directly to the source research.
  • Content Creation: Find credible, citable evidence to support claims in articles, reports, or presentations.
  • Decision Making: Make evidence-based decisions by quickly gathering relevant scientific findings on a subject.

Getting Started: A Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Go to the Website: Navigate to the `consensus.app` website.
  2. Ask a Research Question: The search bar is optimized for research questions. Instead of keywords, ask a question as if you were asking a researcher. For example: "Does intermittent fasting improve metabolic health?"
  3. Review the Results: Consensus will show you a list of "cards," each containing a direct quote or finding from a research paper that addresses your question. It often categorizes them (e.g., "Yes," "No," "Possibly").
  4. Use the Synthesizer: At the top, you'll often see a "Summary" or "Synthesize" feature. This provides an AI-generated summary of the findings from the top-cited papers, giving you a quick overview of the scientific consensus.
  5. Explore the Papers: Each card links directly to the source paper, often with an open-access PDF if available, so you can dive deeper into the research.

Tips for Effective Use

  • Ask Questions, Not Keywords: You will get much better results by phrasing your query as a question (e.g., "What is the effect of..." or "Is there a link between...").
  • Use the Filters: You can filter results by study type (e.g., Randomized Controlled Trial, Meta-Analysis), journal quality, and other criteria to find the highest-quality evidence.
  • Look for Meta-Analyses: For many topics, a meta-analysis or systematic review provides the strongest level of evidence. Use the filters to find these first.
  • Check the Context: The extracted claims are powerful, but always click through to the paper to understand the full context of the study before drawing strong conclusions.
